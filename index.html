Greening Uganda's Dry Cattle Corridor

The Dry Cattle Corridor Initiative partners with farmers and local communities to restore degraded lands, improve soil fertility, and build climate resilience.

The Problem

Uganda’s dry cattle corridor—stretching across Kiruhura, Lyantonde, Ssembabule, Nakaseke, Nakasongola, and Luweero—faces severe land degradation worsened by drought, deforestation, and overgrazing.

Our Approach

We restore farms and empower pastoral families by focusing on:

  • Soil and water conservation practices
  • Distribution of drought-resistant tree species
  • Community-led reforestation drives
  • Training farmers in sustainable land management

Our Impact

  • Improved soil moisture and fertility
  • Increased fodder availability
  • Greater climate resilience among pastoral households
  • Restoration of biodiversity and natural vegetation

Recommended Tree Species

  • Acacia species
  • Calliandra
  • Grevillea robusta
  • Ficus natalensis
  • Mangifera indica (Mango)
  • Markhamia lutea
