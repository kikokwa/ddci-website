Restoring Life in Uganda’s Dry Cattle Corridor

Reforesting farms, restoring soils, empowering pastoral communities.

Join the Movement

The Problem

Uganda’s dry cattle corridor—spanning Kiruhura, Lyantonde, Ssembabule, Nakasongola, Nakaseke, and Luweero—faces accelerated land degradation. Overgrazing, climate change, and continuous droughts have led to severe soil loss, reduced fodder, and diminishing livestock productivity.

Our Approach

DCCI partners with farmers and local leaders to reforest degraded farms, restore soils, and build resilience through climate-smart techniques. Our work includes:

  • Farmer training on soil and water conservation
  • Distribution of drought-tolerant tree species
  • Farm mapping and restoration planning
  • Community reforestation drives and youth engagement

Our Impact

Through targeted interventions, we help communities achieve:

  • Increased fodder availability
  • Improved soil fertility and moisture retention
  • Reduced vulnerability to droughts
  • Restored biodiversity and greener landscapes

Recommended Tree Species for Restoration

  • Acacia species
  • Calliandra
  • Grevillea robusta
  • Ficus natalensis
  • Mangifera indica (Mango)
  • Markhamia lutea
